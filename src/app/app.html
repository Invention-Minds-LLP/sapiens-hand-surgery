What Is Hand Surgery?
Hand surgery is a specialized branch of orthopedics focused on treating conditions affecting the hand, wrist, fingers, and forearm. It addresses problems involving bones, joints, tendons, ligaments, nerves, and soft tissues that impact hand strength, movement, and coordination.
The goal of hand surgery is to relieve pain, restore function, and improve hand mobility using the safest and most effective treatment approach. Depending on the condition, treatment may include fracture fixation, tendon repair, nerve surgery, or corrective procedures using advanced microsurgical techniques.
Not all hand conditions require surgery. A thorough evaluation at a dedicated hand orthopedic clinic helps determine whether conservative treatment or surgical intervention is appropriate.
Symptoms - When to Visit a Hand Surgeon?
You should consult a hand surgeon if you experience:
  • Persistent hand or wrist pain
  • Swelling or deformity after injury
  • Difficulty moving fingers or gripping objects
  • Numbness, tingling or loss of sensation
  • Pain or stiffness affecting daily activities or work
  • Hand bone fracture or injury not healing properly
  • Weakness of the hand or fingers
Hand Surgery Recommended For?
Hand surgery may be advised for patients who:
  • Have hand bone fractures requiring surgical stabilization
  • Experience tendon or ligament injuries
  • Have nerve compression or nerve damage in the hand
  • Suffer from chronic hand pain or stiffness
  • Have hand injuries due to accidents, falls, or sports trauma
  • Require precise hand function for professional or daily activities
